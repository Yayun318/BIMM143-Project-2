Scientific Question: Since the research shows that the MyD88-dependent TLR signaling pathway is most likely to be functional in terms of Mycobacterium Vaccae vaccine effect, is it possible for Mycobacterium Vaccae to have the same effect of stimulating human TLR signaling pathway?¶

Toll-like receptor 2 also known as TLR2 is a protein that in humans is encoded by the TLR2 gene. TLR2 is one of the toll-like receptors and plays a role in the immune system. TLR2 is a membrane protein, a receptor, which is expressed on the surface of certain cells and recognizes foreign substances and passes on appropriate signals to the cells of the immune system. (Rock et al., 1998)

TLR-mediated MyD88-dependent signaling pathway. MyD88 binds to the cytoplasmic portion of TLRs through interaction between individual TIR domains.(Takeda, 2004)

In a recent research, M. vaccae vaccination was shown to have a protection effect against M. tuberculosis infection. Further analysis pinpointed to the MyD88-dependent TLR signaling pathway and PI3K-Akt signaling pathway as most likely to be functional. doi: 10.1186/s40779-020-00258-4 https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7268289/

Scientific Hypothesis: If the TLR2 receptor of humans is structurally similar to the TLR2 receptor of mice, Mycobacterium Vaccae vaccine might have the same effect of stimulating TLR signaling pathway in humans.¶

To test the structural similarity between Human and Mouse TLR2 receptors, we first need to align the TLR2 gene sequence of the two species through pariwise sequence. We will also align human and mouse gene sequences with pig and mouse through multiple sequence alignment to rule out random chances that human and mouse share similar sequences in the evolutional progresses. We will get our gene sequences data from NCBI website and download them as fasta file. Then, we will visualize the alignment results through Sequence LOGOS. If there are many shared bases in the sequences, we can assume that Human TLR2 and Mouse TLR2 are structually similar.

In addition, we will use homology sequence to get the protein structure of Human TLR2 and Mouse TLR2. We will get our protein sequences from NCBI website and download them as fasta file. Then we can use 3D protein measurement to compare different properties of the two species: amino acid sequence, secondary structure (hexlix, turn, and sheet), hydrophobicity.

If these properties are all similar, we could get to the conclusion that TLR2 receptor of humans is structurally similar to the TLR2 receptor of mice, and thus Mycobacterium Vaccae vaccine might have the same effect of stimulating TLR signaling pathway in huamans.

Performing Bioinformatics Analysis 1: Multiple Sequence Alignment¶

Definition: Multiple sequence alignment (MSA) is a tool used to identify the evolutionary relationships and common patterns between genes. It is generally the alignment of three or more biological sequences (protein or nucleic acid) of similar length. In our case, we want to align the TLR2 genes of Human, Mouse, Pig, and Dog, so that in the next step we can use sequence Logos to figure out the similarity between the genes.

for filename in glob('*.fna'):
    with open(filename) as f:
        # Create an empty file with the file name added with '-aligned.fna'.
        output = str(filename)
        output += '-aligned.fna'
        # The input for Mafft alignment is the string of the filename.
        in_file = str(filename)
        mafft_cline = MafftCommandline(input=in_file)
        stdout, stderr = mafft_cline()
        # Rewrite the output of the Mafft multiple sequence alignment results to the empty file that we created.
        with open(output, 'w') as handle:
            handle.write(stdout)

Plotting the Results 1: Sequence Logos¶

Definition: A sequence logo is created from a collection of aligned sequences (from above) and depicts the consensus sequence and diversity of the sequences. It is a great way to visualize large amounts of DNA or protein sequences that share in common a conserved feature. To read the sequence logos, the larger the letter (which represents the bases of DNA sequences) is, the higher frequency the sequences share at the point.

Analyzing the Results¶

In the multiple sequence alignment, we align the sequence among human, mouse, pig, and dog, and then use sequence LOGOS to visualize two segments: first 50 sequences and 1000~1050th seqeunces. Since both the two segments have few shared bases (the same base at one point), the results demonstrate a relatively disparity between Human and Mouse TLR2 gene sequences.

Homology modeling refers to constructing an atomic-resolution model of the "target" protein from its amino acid sequence and an experimental three-dimensional structure of a related homologous protein. By using SWISS Model, we got that the sequence similarity is 0.48 and the coverage is 0.69. Then we use 3D protein measurements to know the specific properties of human and mouse TLR protein. The significant gaps in amino acid sequence percent, structure fraction, and hydrophocbicity between human and mouse TLR protein all demonstrate the different properties between the two proteins.

Overall, the significant genetic and structural differences between human and mouse TLR2 indicate that Mycobacterium Vaccae vaccine would not have the same effect of stimulating TLR signaling pathway in humans.
